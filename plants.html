PLANTS

Offensive Plants

Plant Name Image Notes
Peashooter Most common plant encountered, and first plant discovered to help fight against zombies. Shoots deadly peas straight forward. Solid attack, can hold off a few zombies on its own. Needs help clearing tougher zombies and hordes. Has many variants, most of which are included.
Double Peashooter Also very common. Very similar to Peashooter, but has double the firing rate. However, it takes up 2x the maintenance. An advantage could be taking up less space than 2 Peashooters.
Ice Peashooter Another common variant of Peashooter. Fires ice cold peas, but requires more maintenance than base Peashooter. Zombies in Toronto seem to be less affected by ice than those observed in other regions, possibly due to Canada's colder climate.
Triple Peashooter Less common variant of Peashooter. Has three heads that act independently, allowing each head to fire a pea at the same rate as a regular Peashooter. Requires over 3x the maintenance of regular Peashooter, yet takes up 1/3 of the space of 3 Peashooters.
Cabbage-Pult Lobs deadly cabbages in an arc. Same maintenance as Peashooter. Can lob cabbages over obstacles, making it useful with blockades. Damage output uncertain when compared to Peashooter.
Corn-Pult Lobs kernels and butter in air. Butter has unknown properties that causes zombies to temporarily halt in place. Same maintenance as Peashooter. Damage output is slightly lower, but stun may be worth it. It is unknown why the Corn-Pult lobs butter in tandem with corn kernels. Leading theory suspects popcorn.
Melon-Pult Lobs heavy watermelons in air. Extremely heavy damage sustained to zombies. High maintenance. Has an ice variant, similar to Peashooter, that also slows zombies down. Ice variant has even higher maintenance requirements.
Potato Land Mine When planted, takes some time to arm itself, then explodes on contact, similar to a land mine. Extremely high damage output. Little to no maintenance required, aside from its own arm time. It is unknown how the potato combusts to create an explosion.
Starfruit Shoots stars out of all 5 appendages. Damage output unknown when compared to Peashooter. Maintenance slightly higher than Peashooter. Not much else is known about it.
Corn Cannon Charges up and fires an extremely deadly ear of corn. Effective crowd control weapon, as the corn makes a small explosion on impact. Extremely high maintenance, but extremely high damage output. Very cool.

Defensive Plants

Plant Name Image Notes
Wall-Nut Solid defensive plant, able to hold back zombies for a long period of time. Often used as a blockade to halt zombies at a location, giving time and space for people to think and act. Appears to be in distress as the zombies chip away at it. Could be worth investigating.
Tall-nut Taller variant of Wall-Nut. Tougher, and blocks some zombies like the Pole-Vaulting Zombie from simply jumping over it, due to its height.
Pumpkin Shell Allows a plant to hide in the inside of its shell, allowing it to protect said plant. Toughness unknown in comparison to Wall-nut. May have potential for humans to hide in it as well.

Support Plants

Plant Name Image Notes
Sunflower Most important function is aid in maintenance of other plants. Vital to any zombie defense blockade. Can surprisingly sing.
Firewood Allows peas that go through it to become fire peas, dealing significantly more damage to zombies. Also illuminates surroundings, due to the nature of fire. It is unclear where the perpetual fire comes from. Pumpkins work great in tandem with these, as these have to be near the front lines.
Wind Clover Creates a strong wind current that blows away Balloon Zombies, as well as the fog. It is unknown how such strong wind currents are created. It is also up for debate whether a 4 leaf variant exists.
